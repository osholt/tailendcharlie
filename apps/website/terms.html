About the service

Tail End Charlie helps a group of riders share a route, hand off a second-bike drop-off marker, and see each other's position and status during a ride, including through a local device-to-device relay when mobile coverage is unavailable.

The website ride planner lets you choose stops, request a road-following route, create a temporary code for loading or reopening it, and download a GPX file for import into the app or another compatible navigation tool. Anyone with a route code or emailed route link can access that shared route until it expires, so you are responsible for sharing it only with people you trust.

Optional motorcycle discovery layers show community or open-data road highlights and destinations. These are descriptive planning aids, not safety endorsements or instructions to ride at a particular speed. Roads may be restricted, closed, damaged or unsuitable, so you must check current signs, access, weather and conditions yourself. Suggestions are reviewed before publication and must not be copied from proprietary maps, route collections or guidebooks without permission that allows reuse.

The app is currently in active development and controlled testing. It is not a certified emergency-coordination tool, and it should not be relied on as the sole way to stay safe, stay together, or reach help in an emergency. Always carry an independent way to call for help, and follow the rules of the road above anything shown in the app.
